Active and Passive Voice

📘 What is Voice? Voice is the form of a verb that shows whether the subject of a sentence does the action or receives the action.

1. Active vs Passive Voice

  • Active Voice: Subject performs the action.
    Example: The cat (subject) chased (verb) the mouse (object).
  • Passive Voice: Subject receives the action.
    Example: The mouse (subject) was chased (verb) by the cat (agent).

2. When to Use Passive Voice?

  • When the doer is unknown, unimportant, or obvious.
  • To emphasise the receiver of the action.
  • In formal or scientific writing.

3. Basic Conversion Rules

  1. Identify the subject, verb, and object in the active sentence.
  2. Move the object to the position of the subject.
  3. Change the verb to its past participle (V3) and add the appropriate form of the auxiliary verb "be" (according to the tense of the active verb).
  4. Place the subject of the active sentence after the preposition "by" (optional, often omitted).
  5. Ensure the new subject agrees with the verb.

4. Tense-wise Conversion Table

Tense Active Verb Form Passive Verb Form Example (Active → Passive)
Simple Present V1 (s/es) is/am/are + V3 She writes a letter. → A letter is written by her.
Present Continuous is/am/are + V1+ing is/am/are + being + V3 She is writing a letter. → A letter is being written by her.
Present Perfect has/have + V3 has/have + been + V3 She has written a letter. → A letter has been written by her.
Simple Past V2 was/were + V3 She wrote a letter. → A letter was written by her.
Past Continuous was/were + V1+ing was/were + being + V3 She was writing a letter. → A letter was being written by her.
Past Perfect had + V3 had + been + V3 She had written a letter. → A letter had been written by her.
Simple Future will/shall + V1 will/shall + be + V3 She will write a letter. → A letter will be written by her.
Future Perfect will/shall + have + V3 will/shall + have + been + V3 She will have written a letter. → A letter will have been written by her.

Note: Future Continuous, Present Perfect Continuous, Past Perfect Continuous, and Future Perfect Continuous are not usually converted to passive.

5. Special Cases and Tricky Constructions

A. Modal Auxiliaries (can, could, may, might, must, ought to, should, etc.)

Rule: Active: modal + V1 → Passive: modal + be + V3

You must finish the work. → The work must be finished (by you).

B. Imperative Sentences (Orders, Requests, Advice)

Rule: Begin with "Let" + object + be + V3, or use "You are requested/ordered/advised to + V1".

Open the door. → Let the door be opened. OR You are ordered to open the door.
Please help me. → You are requested to help me.

C. Interrogative Sentences

  • Yes/No Questions: Start the passive with the auxiliary verb.
    Did she write the letter? → Was the letter written by her?
  • Wh-Questions: Keep the Wh-word at the beginning, then add the passive structure.
    Who wrote this book? → Who was this book written by? OR By whom was this book written?
    Why did she write this letter? → Why was this letter written by her?

D. Sentences with Two Objects (Direct and Indirect)

Some verbs like give, send, offer, show, teach, promise take two objects. Two passives are possible.

Active: She gave me a book.
Passive 1 (indirect object as subject): I was given a book (by her).
Passive 2 (direct object as subject): A book was given to me (by her).

E. Sentences with Prepositions

The preposition remains with the verb in passive.

Active: The nurse looked after the patient. → Passive: The patient was looked after by the nurse.
Active: They laughed at him. → Passive: He was laughed at (by them).

F. Sentences with "Let" (Suggestions, Permissions)

Active: Let him do it. → Passive: Let it be done by him.
Active: They let him go. → Passive: He was let go. (rare; usually: He was allowed to go.)

G. Sentences with Infinitives (to + V1)

Active: She wants to buy a car. → Passive: She wants a car to be bought.
Active: I have to finish the work. → Passive: The work has to be finished by me.

H. Exclamatory Sentences

Exclamations are rarely converted, but if needed, treat them as assertive.

Active: What a beautiful picture she painted! → Passive: What a beautiful picture was painted by her! (awkward but possible)

I. Sentences with Reflexive/Emphatic Pronouns (myself, yourself, etc.)

Usually not converted. If needed, use "by + pronoun".

Active: He hurt himself. → Passive: He was hurt by himself. (rare)

J. Sentences with "Need", "Want", "Require" (followed by gerund)

Active: The car needs repairing. → Passive: The car needs to be repaired.

K. Sentences with "No" (Negative)

Active: Nobody saw him. → Passive: He was not seen by anybody. OR He was seen by nobody.
Active: He did nothing. → Passive: Nothing was done by him.

L. Sentences with "It" as Impersonal Subject

Active: People say that he is a genius. → Passive: It is said that he is a genius. OR He is said to be a genius.

6. Common Errors to Avoid

  • Using the wrong form of "be".
  • Forgetting to change the verb to V3.
  • Omitting the preposition when required.
  • Using "by" with unnecessary agents.
  • Mixing up active and passive in complex sentences.

7. Practice Questions (Tricky Ones)

  1. Who broke the window?
  2. Please give me a glass of water.
  3. Let her sing a song.
  4. They made him the captain.
  5. The baby was laughing at the clown.
  6. Did you see the movie?
  7. Someone has stolen my purse.
  8. The teacher explained the lesson to us.
  9. You must not park your car here.
  10. I saw him crossing the road.
  11. He is known to me. (Already passive? Think!)
  12. What did you do?
  13. Let us play cricket.
  14. Why did she laugh at you?
  15. They are building a new hospital.
  16. He loves chocolates.
  17. Who will take care of the baby?
  18. People believe that he is a spy.
  19. He had never cheated anyone.
  20. Please don't disturb me.

Answers

  1. By whom was the window broken? / Who was the window broken by?
  2. You are requested to give me a glass of water. / Let a glass of water be given to me.
  3. Let a song be sung by her.
  4. He was made the captain (by them).
  5. The clown was being laughed at by the baby.
  6. Was the movie seen by you?
  7. My purse has been stolen.
  8. We were explained the lesson by the teacher. / The lesson was explained to us by the teacher.
  9. Your car must not be parked here.
  10. He was seen crossing the road by me.
  11. If given "He is known to me", it's already passive. (Active: I know him.)
  12. What was done by you?
  13. Let cricket be played by us.
  14. Why were you laughed at by her?
  15. A new hospital is being built by them.
  16. Chocolates are loved by him.
  17. By whom will the baby be taken care of? / Who will the baby be taken care of by?
  18. It is believed that he is a spy. / He is believed to be a spy.
  19. No one had ever been cheated by him. / Anyone had never been cheated by him.
  20. You are requested not to disturb me.
💡 Pro Tip: For exams, always check if the sentence has two objects, a preposition, or a modal – these are common traps. Practice daily!
